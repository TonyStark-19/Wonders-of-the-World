Facts about Colosseum
  • The Colosseum is an ancient Roman amphitheater, the largest ever built, with a capacity of up to 80,000 spectators.
  • It was commissioned by Emperor Vespasian in AD 72 and completed by his son Titus in AD 80.
  • Gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and mock sea battles were held in the Colosseum.
  • The structure is made of concrete and sand and features a complex system of vaults and arches.
  • It is a symbol of the Roman Empire and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
