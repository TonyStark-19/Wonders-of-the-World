Facts about Chichén Itzá
  • Chichen Itza was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya civilization, located on the Yucatan Peninsula.
  • Its most famous structure is El Castillo (Temple of Kukulcán), a step pyramid with 365 steps, symbolizing the calendar year.
  • The site features various architectural styles, reflecting a fusion of Maya and Toltec influences.
  • Chichen Itza was an important center for trade, politics, and religion during the Late Classic period (600–900 AD).
  • The city includes other structures like the Great Ball Court and the Temple of the Warriors.
